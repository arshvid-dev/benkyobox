**Okimasu** (起きる / おきます) **"to wake up"** (起きる) 

---

### **The Conjugation Table**

| Form | Japanese (Polite) | English Meaning |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Present** | Okimasu | I wake up / I will wake up |
| **Presumptive** | Okiru deshō | I probably will wake up |
| **Imperative** | Okinasai / Okire | Wake up! (Command) |
| **Past Indicative** | Okimashita | I woke up |
| **Past Presumptive** | Okitaderashō | I probably woke up |
| **Present Progressive** | Okite imasu | I am waking up / I am awake |
| **Past Progressive** | Okite imashita | I was waking up / I was awake |
| **Provisional (Ba)** | Okireba | If I wake up... |
| **Conditional (Tara)** | Okitara | If/When I wake up... |

| **Causative** | Okisasemasu | To make/let someone wake up |
| **Passive** | Okiraremasu | To be woken up (often by someone) |

### **Detailed Explanations & Examples**

#### **1. Present & Presumptive**

* **Present:** Used for habits or future intent.
* *Example:* "I wake up at 6 AM." → **6-ji ni okimasu.**


* **Presumptive:** Expresses a guess or probability.
* *Example:* "He will probably wake up soon." → **Kare wa sugu okiru deshō.**



#### **2. Imperative (The Command)**

Used to give orders. *Okinasai* is firm but polite (like a parent to a child), while *Okire* is very blunt/harsh.

* *Example:* "Wake up now!" → **Ima sugu okinasai!**

#### **3. Past (Indicative & Presumptive)**

* **Past Indicative:** A completed action.
* *Example:* "I woke up early today." → **Kyō wa hayaku okimashita.**


* **Past Presumptive:** Guessing about something that already happened.
* *Example:* "He probably woke up already." → **Mō okitadeshō.**



#### **4. Progressive (The "-ing" forms)**

These use the **Te-form** (*Okite*) + *imasu*.

* **Present Progressive:** "I am currently waking up." → **Ima okite imasu.**
* **Past Progressive:** "I was waking up when you called." → **Denwa ga atta toki, okite imashita.**

#### **5. Conditionals (If/When)**

* **Provisional (~ba):** Focuses on the condition itself.
* *Example:* "If you wake up, let’s go." → **Okireba, ikimashō.**


* **Conditional (~tara):** More common in speech; implies "after" or "if."
* *Example:* "When you wake up, drink water." → **Okitara, mizu o nonde kudasai.**



#### **6. Potential, Causative, & Passive**

* **Potential (Ability):** "I can wake up early." → **Hayaku okiraremasu.**
* **Causative (Making someone do it):** "I made the child wake up." → **Kodomo o okisasemashita.**
* **Passive (Action done to you):** In Japanese, this is often used "adversively" (someone woke you up and it was annoying).
* *Example:* "I was woken up by the cat." → **Neko ni okiraremashita.**
